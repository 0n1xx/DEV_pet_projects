    About Us

    Introduction

    At 2025 Sony Walkman Shop, we are dedicated to reviving the golden era of portable music, one Walkman at a time. Our passion lies in celebrating and preserving the legacy of one of the most iconic inventions in audio history — the Sony Walkman.

    Our History

    1979 – The Birth of the Walkman

    Sony unveiled the first-ever portable cassette player: the Sony Walkman TPS-L2. For the first time, people could carry music in their pockets, plug in headphones, and enter their own private soundscape. It was more than a device — it was a cultural revolution.

    1980s–1990s – The Golden Era

    The Walkman defined youth culture, music portability, and self-expression. From cassettes to CDs and MiniDiscs, it adapted to the times while staying true to its essence: music, anywhere, anytime.

    2000s–2020s – Digital Takeover

    As streaming and smartphones took over, physical media faded into the background. But the charm, character, and physicality of the Walkman never truly disappeared.

    2025 – Rewind, Rediscover

    In a fast, intangible digital world, a group of collectors and sound lovers in Toronto asked: What if we brought the Walkman back — not just as a product, but as an experience? We started in a small loft, surrounded by cassette tapes and curiosity. Word spread. Musicians, tech fans, and nostalgic teens came together.

    We restored Walkmans, built relationships with former Sony engineers, sourced rare parts, and created custom designs. Some devices came from Tokyo markets, others from Berlin attics. We gave each a second life.

    Today, the 2025 Sony Walkman Shop is a global hub for analog audio lovers — a repair lab, a cultural space, and a love letter to the past that still speaks to the future.

    Our Mission

    To keep the Walkman legacy alive by offering high-quality products, expert knowledge, and a vibrant community for everyone who loves music — not just hearing it, but feeling it.
